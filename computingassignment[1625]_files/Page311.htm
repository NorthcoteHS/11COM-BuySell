Possible Solutions

SOLUTION 1: When customers cannot pay, provide an unpaid employment for a period of time.

We suggest is when customers are unable to pay the loan(s) or they have bankrupt, the bank will provide them an unpaid employment for a period of time. This means that customers would become the banks part-time or full-time workers, but their salary will go directly to the bank to pay their loan. The advantages of this solution is customers would have an option other than directly paying back their loan. Also the bank would have more free workers. However, the disadvantages of this solution is there would be fewer customers pay their loans.directly and therefore less income for the banks.

SOLUTION 2: The bank can lower the interest rate.
From this solution. the bank would get more attention and more customers. This solution would be benefit to both stakeholders: banks and customers. Banks would earn more money because more customers would be able to pay their loans. However, this would not influence investors to invest to banks, therefore banks may also get bankruptcy. Moreover, this solution is not practical at all because the banks cannot lower the interest rate by their own.

SOLUTION 3: The government should implement some laws to protect the rights of customers when lending money:

If the government implement some new regulations, the banks definitely have to obey that and therefore customers would get more benefits and rights. However, the banks would be restricted to what they can do, therefore they would not be able to make much money as before the new regulations.

SOLUTION 4: The banks should have to warn customers of any risks associated with their actions and warn them of possible incoming difficulties. This would have a customer service department where customers can seek advice from a credit consultant about their lending for example, whether or not they should stop, how high their income should to be able to pay their loan(s) plus the interest, etc. That department will also in charge of warning customers about the risks they could get if they keep borrowing and some possible income difficulties. The advantages of this solution is customers can trust the banks. They would be able to be more aware of the risks they are taking and make more informed decisions on what they should be doing with their money. They also aren’t pushed by banks into positions that are financially unstable. The disadvantage of this solution is the banks would have to employ more credit consultants, also the banks would have to restrict what they can do, possibly impacting revenue.
