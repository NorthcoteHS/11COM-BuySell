Government

Since the nation’s economy is greatly affected by the bank, there are some pros and cons that the government have in this issue. First of all, as part of the bank income goes to the tax, the government will gain more if the bank makes more. More specifically, The new tax — or levy — is a tax on each banks' liabilities and is calculated quarterly at 0.015 per cent which gives an annual rate of 0.06 per cent. It is expected the levy will raise between $1.5 billion and $1.6 billion each year, for four years. So roughly $6.2 billion in the forward estimates ( Clarke, C. (2018). The government can then use that money to improve the country’s living standard, for example, healthcare, transportation, education, etc. Therefore, when the government has money, they can easily control the economy of the country.
